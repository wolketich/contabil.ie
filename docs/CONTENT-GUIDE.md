# Content guide

## Audience

Primary audience:

- Romanian and Moldovan people living and working in Ireland
- Construction subcontractors using RCT
- New self-employed workers and freelancers
- People with missing or late annual returns
- Self-employed people preparing mortgage applications

Company owners are not the primary audience. Company formation remains a secondary service.

## Brand voice

- Friendly and conversational
- Familiar and reassuring
- Direct about consequences
- Clear about the next step
- Never judgemental
- Never overly formal

The emotional starting point is: “I feel lost and need a familiar person to explain what to do.”

## Conversion structure

Each important page should follow this order:

1. Name the problem in the visitor's words.
2. State a factual consequence.
3. Explain that the problem can be reviewed and organised.
4. Give one immediate action, usually WhatsApp.
5. Add a source for factual risk claims.
6. Explain the service and required documents.
7. Answer uncomfortable questions.
8. Repeat the free first-conversation action.

## Ethical urgency

Use specific facts such as the current RCT rates or published late-filing surcharges. Do not use invented deadlines, false scarcity, humiliation, or claims that the service guarantees a Revenue or bank outcome.

## Language

Romanian copy should sound natural to Romanian and Moldovan residents in Ireland. Irish terms such as Revenue, ROS, RCT, Form 11, mortgage, self-employed and Tax Clearance may remain in English when that is how clients commonly recognise them.
